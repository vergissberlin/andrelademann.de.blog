You may have read the other articles in my series on design principles. SOLID is one of the principles for which I need more words than usual. In fact, it is not just one principle — SOLID comprises 5! Each letter stands for one principle.

In my experience, this is a common question in job interviews in Germany: which programming paradigms do you know? SOLID should be among them.

So let's take a closer look at _SOLID_. Unlike other posts on this topic, I will only make a small introduction and then try to explain it with a meme and an example. Hopefully this will make it easier to remember.

## S – stands for

**… Single Responsibility Principle.** In programming, the Single Responsibility Principle states that every module or class should have responsibility over a single part of the functionality provided by the software.
